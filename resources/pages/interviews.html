Question
1 Who is the target audience for this system?
2 How would users interact with this system?
3 What features would make this application usable?
4 What would be the best way to learn the interface?
5 Where would you like to access specific features?
6 What personal characteristics would you like this app to make matches with?
7 What tools outside of this application would work well?
8 How would you like to use communication within the application?
9 How often would you feel appropriate searching for games or people?
10 What kind of time limit would you put on matchmaking?
11 What kind of error messages would be the most friendly?
12 In your opinion, with which types of games do you think this application would work well?

Interviewee: Troy Mathews

Question # Answer
1 Anyone who plays any kind of game. Rated E for everyone. Except for maybe old people.
2 You could place what could be considered an ad listing for whatever game you want to play. Afterwards, you could put in more information regarding what king of game, what kind of materials are required, what kind of people you would like to play with.
3 The mass variety of games that you could find. Anybody can use this app to find or host any game of any kind. Whether you want to play poker, D&D, or hangman, it's the variety that gets people.
4 The first time you use any feature in the app, then it will show you how to perform each step. Then you would know how to do it every time afterward. Have this tutorial be real, don't have it make fake transactions. All tutorial transactions will actually be real.
5 The main page would have a dropdown menu where you could pick a feature category, such ase achievements, invites, etc. All of these should open within the app, and not redirect through chrome or whatever.
6 Obviously you would need some kind of profile page, and maybe some kind of questionairre with random questions. Maybe it scores you somehow, and pits you with other people with similar scores. A profile page would be cool, because then you can can if someone is a D-Bag.
7 Have a subreddit for it, and a website that functions very similar to the app. Traffic to the website could definitely help ad revenue.
8 Not only a message board that separates from the website & the app, but also a facetime-like integration that could help communicate with other members to make sure that they are on the same page, and not D-bags.
9 Maybe making it easier to refine a search, that way it takes less time to find something that interests you. I think if it took longer than a minute to find a game or people to play with, I'd get pretty annoyed. I think the refined search should include stuff like type of game (such as tabletop), number of members playing, and days intended to play to help make searching better, faster, and easier to use.
10 I think that a minute would be plenty of time to find a quick game to play. Assuming that this application is trying to find people to play with right now, but it is using personal statistics and characteristics to match players, a minute would be acceptable.
11 Is none a correct answer? I'd like that. But seriously, let's say you refined your search too much, I think it should just say something polite like, “Maybe you should be less specific?”
12 Games that you would need a little more setup with. Like Dungeons and Dragons, etc, where like, you have to take days off work to go play in person and take hours. I'm not saying that you shouldn't play Halo where you can find a match in an instant, but it's definitely more suitable for table-top style games. Games that need setup, where details would need hashed out.
